★ Honor ★ Courage ★ Commitment ★

My name is Avery White. My parents are Karen White from District 3, Gila River Indian Community and Jess White from Sisseton, South Dakota. My maternal grandparents are the late Lawrence and Lolita Jackson from District 3, Gila River Indian Community. My paternal grandparents are the late Gary and Pearl White from Popular, Montana and Sisseton, South Dakota. I am a United States Marine Corps veteran (2000-2004). I am the Sr. Vice Commander of the Haskell Osife-Antone, American Legion, Post 51. I have respectfully held the positions of Secretary III, Legislative Assistant, Administrative Assistant to the Planning & Zoning Commission, Enrollment Committee, Law Enforcement Citizen’s Advisory Board, Economic Development and Health & Social Standing Committees. My commitment and volunteer work in the Gila River Indian Community include: Twelve years with the Mul-Chu-Tha, Battle of the Bands Committee, seven years as the Executive Director for team Outkast, coaching youth ages 6-16yo in tee-ball, baseball and fast pitch softball. I have served one term on the District Three Recreation Committee. After accomplishing these goals I reprioritized to spending quality time with my wife of 3 years and our 5 wonderful kids. Eight years ago I ran in my first Council election an Elder ask me a simple question “why are you running for Council?” As a young man I responded “because I want to make a difference.” His response was, “why do you need to be a Councilman to do that?” Since that conversation I set out to make a difference in the Community as a person not requiring a title to do things but being a leader and being the change I wanted to see. It is why I declared my candidacy, because you can’t continue to do the same thing and expect a different outcome.
